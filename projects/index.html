Selected Projects

Enzyme Design and Computational Enzymology

  • ROADMAP for catalytic activity landscape modeling and adaptive enzyme engineering. This project addresses the challenge of predicting and adapting enzyme activity across sequence, substrate, pH, and temperature in sparse experimental settings. It delivered a unified AI framework that jointly models kcat, Km, and kcat/Km with calibrated confidence and supports rapid adaptation to new enzyme systems. ROADMAP outperformed five representative methods in computational benchmarks and enabled low-budget wet-lab engineering campaigns with fewer than 10 assayed variants on average, mean hit rates above 30%, and mean activity improvements of 4-fold. My contribution was leading the first-author project, designing the contrastive and adaptive-learning framework, building the benchmark datasets, and driving translation from model development to iterative experimental validation.
  • EnzyControl for substrate-aware enzyme backbone generation. This project addresses the inability of existing enzyme-generation methods to simultaneously preserve catalytic function, enforce substrate specificity, and maintain backbone designability. It delivered EnzyBind, a curated benchmark of 11,100 enzyme-substrate pairs with functional-site annotations, and a generation framework that improved designability and catalytic efficiency by 13% while producing more compact backbones with better EC consistency and binding-related performance. My contribution was co-leading the research direction as co-corresponding author, shaping the problem formulation and model strategy, and mentoring the project from dataset design through evaluation and paper delivery.
  • Protein-sequence-aware enzymatic reaction prediction. This project addresses the dependence of traditional enzymatic reaction prediction on templates or EC annotations, which limits generalization to poorly annotated proteins. It produced an end-to-end model that directly maps protein sequence and substrate structure to reaction outcome, supported by a curated reaction resource of more than 30,000 standardized records expanded to million-scale training samples through augmentation, with performance comparable to EC-input models. My contribution was leading the model conception, database integration, and the early development of sequence-to-reaction enzyme intelligence.

Protein Conformation and Macromolecular Modeling

  • ProteinConformers for conformational landscape modeling and benchmarking. This project addresses the lack of large-scale resources that cover non-native to near-native protein conformational landscapes together with energetic annotations and rigorous evaluation standards. It delivered a resource of 2.7 million geometry-optimized conformations, 13.7 million energy evaluations, and 5.5 million similarity annotations across hundreds of proteins, together with new metrics for atom-level plausibility and landscape coverage and a community-facing platform for benchmarking generators. My contribution was leading the first-author program end-to-end, including problem framing, multi-seed molecular-dynamics pipeline design, large-scale HPC sampling, dataset curation, metric development, and benchmarking strategy.
  • ONION for environment-aware inverse folding from monomers to complexes. This project addresses the fragmented nature of inverse-folding methods, which typically model only one molecular modality at a time and ignore biochemical context in heterogeneous assemblies. It delivered a unified all-atom framework for proteins, DNA, and RNA that conditions sequence design on environmental context and achieves better to competitive performance on monomers and complexes with improved sequence and structural fidelity compared to ProteinMPNN, LigandMPNN, and NA-MPNN. My contribution was leading the first-author program, defining the unified problem setting, shaping the model architecture and evaluation scope, and driving the manuscript and benchmarking effort.
  • DPStab for mutation-induced stability change prediction. This project addresses the weakness of sequence-based mutation models in capturing local structural perturbations around the mutated residue. It produced a model that combines a protein language model, cross-attention over contact changes, self-distillation, and antisymmetric constraints, achieving state-of-the-art prediction of both ΔΔG and ΔTm together with strong ranking on large-scale mutational datasets. My contribution was co-leading experiment design and model discussion, contributing to the technical strategy and manuscript development as co-first author.
  • Protein structure pretraining and differentiable energy modeling. This project addresses the immaturity of structure-based pretraining and the limitations of classical energy functions, which are often non-differentiable and hard to optimize. It established an all-atom structural encoder with long-range interaction modeling, used conformational similarity and potential-energy prediction as pretraining tasks, and derived a deep potential function whose conformational correlations improved relative to traditional energy formulations. My contribution was conceiving the pretraining objectives, linking representation learning to energy modeling, and organizing how these components support downstream structure tasks.
  • High-throughput protein structure prediction platform for industrial discovery. This project addresses the gap between AlphaFold-level accuracy and the throughput needed for industrial antibody and large-molecule discovery workflows. It delivered a parallelized prediction platform with multi-model and multi-parameter support, second-level average turnaround under optimized settings, thousand-scale task submission, and automated parameter recommendation for different protein classes and lengths. My contribution was designing and deploying the platform workflow, optimizing scheduling and infrastructure across Linux and multi-GPU environments, and integrating it into downstream industrial discovery use cases.

Structure-based Molecular Generation

  • DrugRPG for pocket-conditioned 3D molecular generation. This project addresses the tendency of existing diffusion and autoregressive generators to produce chemically invalid molecules or physically implausible poses with severe steric clashes. It delivered a representation-aligned and physics-guided diffusion model that improved binding affinity and ligand efficiency by at least 6%, reduced steric clashes by more than 69%, and substantially improved self-consistency and synthetic accessibility over leading baselines. My contribution was co-leading the project as co-first author, helping design the representation-alignment strategy and physics-guided sampling scheme, and driving the evaluation and manuscript development.
  • PepScape for pocket-conditioned multi-objective peptide generation. This project addresses the difficulty of generating peptide sequences that preserve pocket-conditioned sequence fidelity while simultaneously satisfying binding-affinity and multi-property optimization objectives. It has established a generation strategy achieving state-of-the-art sequence recovery from pocket-conditioned design and enabling joint optimization of binding affinity and developability-related properties. The framework is currently being validated through wet-lab collaboration on cancer targets. My contribution has been shaping the research direction, framing the multi-objective optimization strategy, and connecting the generative framework to downstream experimental validation as co-corresponding author.
  • Flexible docking under receptor and ligand flexibility. This project addresses practical structure-based design settings where receptor flexibility and ligand conformational uncertainty reduce the reliability of standard rigid-receptor docking pipelines. It has established an ongoing framework for more realistic pose prediction and docking evaluation under conformational variability, with the goal of improving applicability in challenging AIDD settings. My contribution has been shaping the research agenda, defining the problem setting and optimization targets, and integrating flexible docking into broader structure-based design workflows as co-first author.
  • Transferable molecular representation learning for virtual screening and optimization. This project addresses the disconnect between molecular representation models used for property prediction and generative models used for controllable design. It produced a unified molecular encoding-decoding framework with state-of-the-art performance in large-scale virtual screening and ten drug-related property tasks, more than 80% high-similarity reconstruction, and successful discovery of three multitarget-active compounds. My contribution was helping define the representation-learning direction, supporting translation toward hit discovery, and manuscript development.

Multi-omics and Genomics Modeling

  • OUTPOST for whole-metagenome shotgun analysis. This project addresses the fragmented nature of metagenomic workflows and the lack of robust support for multi-group experimental designs. It delivered an end-to-end software system with 12 modules, more than 50 analytical functions, more than 20 visualization types, integration of 40+ third-party tools, and new mechanisms for group-stratified comparison, computational perturbation, and meta-analysis-based biomarker discovery. My contribution was first-author leadership on platform architecture, feature design, reproducible workflow integration, and application to real biological datasets.
  • TRIP for large-scale telomeric repeat identification and evolution. This project addresses the fact that telomeric repeat discovery traditionally depends on chromosome-level assemblies and lacks scalable methods for short-read data. It produced an ultra-fast identification pipeline and the largest insect telomeric-repeat survey of its kind, analyzing more than 7 Tbp across 129 species from 21 orders, confirming the ancestral (TTAGG)n repeat and uncovering diverse hymenopteran repeat innovations. My contribution was first-author leadership in method development, large-scale comparative analysis, and evolutionary interpretation and validation.
  • Cross-species single-cell analysis of vertebrate pineal gland evolution. This project addresses the lack of a single-cell, cross-species view of how the pineal gland evolved from photoreceptive to neuroendocrine roles. It integrated 22,431 cells from zebrafish, rat, and monkey datasets to reveal conserved and species-specific features in phototransduction, melatonin synthesis, ligand-receptor signaling, and transcription-factor regulation. My contribution was serving as co-first author on the computational analysis, cross-species integration, and biological interpretation of the evolutionary programs.
  • Single-cell characterization of cerebrospinal-fluid tumor cells in lung-cancer brain metastasis. This project addresses the lack of molecular characterization and diagnostic signatures for circulating tumor cells in the cerebrospinal fluid of leptomeningeal metastasis patients. It established the first single-cell transcriptomic workflow for this setting across six patients, identified marker programs and clinically relevant heterogeneity, and derived a 20-gene candidate signature for digital liquid biopsy development. My contribution was serving as co-first author, leading bioinformatics analysis and helping establish the laboratory’s early single-cell analysis pipeline.
  • Applied metagenomics in gut microbiome dysbiosis studies. This project addressed how fescue toxicosis reshapes the rectum microbiome of beef cattle under a controlled feeding experiment. It delivered whole-genome shotgun metagenomic profiling of fecal samples before and after toxic tall fescue exposure, identified significant reductions in alpha diversity and shifts in beta diversity, and revealed species-level and functional changes including enrichment of Ruminococcaceae bacterium P7 and antimicrobial-resistance-related pathways. My contribution as co-first author was supporting metagenomic analysis, microbial profiling, and biological interpretation in a study that further validated candidate biomarker with experimental validation.
