Projects

2020

Profiles of telomeric repeats in Insecta reveal diverse forms of telomeric motifs in Hymenopterans

key words

telomere; WGS sequencing; bioinformatics; motif; TTATTGGG;

the story

When Dr. Wang firstly introduced me this project, he only wanted to check the telomeric repeat motif in 7 Nasonia species. I finished the analysis and wrote the article. Unfortunately, he was so busy that the MS was left in a forgotten corner for months. Until, we noticed another paper identified the TTATTGGG motif, which was our highlight. Predictably, no one in the lab had confidence or interest in this project anymore. But I never gave up. I expanded the scale from 7 nasonia species to all taxa with public whole-genome sequencing data. I spent months reviewing the database records and articles with eyes, for hundreds of them. Finally, I made the most comprehensive view of telomeric repeat motif phylogenetic distribution to date. I wrote the manuscript 7 times. The manuscript was under review for 359 days, which stands for the longest record in Wang lab. My tutor helped me a lot during the under-review. I even developed a software, TRIP, for this project. This is a bitter but relieved story.

the software

TRIP is a pipeline for identifying the TRM (telomeric repeat motif) using short-read genome sequencing data.

2019

Circulating tumor cell characterization of lung cancer brain metastases in the cerebrospinal fluid through single‐cell transcriptome analysis

key words

single cell sequencing; lung cancer; erebrospinal fluid;

the story

Thank Dr. Wang, Dr. Zhang, Dr. Guan, Mr. Ruan, who helped me during the project. Five batches of graduate students in my lab handled this project before me. But none of them figured out how to process the single cell RNA data systematically. I was the first one to actually forwarded the project to publication level. As a first year graduate student, I learned almost everything by myself, from BASH coding to R Seurat package. My pipeline and scripts initialized, founded, and inspired the tens of single cell work in Zhang lab, until today.
