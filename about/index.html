Curriculum Vitae

About

I am a scientist working on AI-driven molecular modeling and drug discovery, with training across bioinformatics, chemical biology, and biomedical sciences. My work sits at the intersection of machine learning, structural biology, molecular generation, and translational research.

I am currently a Research Fellow at the School of Computing and the Cancer Science Institute, National University of Singapore. My recent research spans protein conformational landscape modeling, inverse folding, structure-based molecular generation, enzyme intelligence, and foundation-model directions for biological data.

Across academia and industry, I have built end-to-end research programs from problem definition and dataset construction to model development, large-scale evaluation, and manuscript delivery. I enjoy work that connects rigorous computational modeling with practical biological questions and, when possible, wet-lab validation.

Research Interests

  • AI for drug discovery and molecular design
  • Protein structure, conformation, and inverse folding
  • Structure-based molecular generation and docking
  • Enzyme modeling and computational enzymology
  • Bioinformatics, multi-omics, and genomics foundation models
