about

Hi there, this is Yihang Zhou.

I’m a dual Ph.D. student in Biomedical Sciences and Bioinformatics Sciences with 10+ academic projects experience. I spent years tackling tough scientific and engineering problems using interdisciplinary knowledge and skills.

My research interests focus on microbiota and synthetic biology. To systematically solve the barriers that hinder the microbiota-based life design, being interdisciplinary is necessary. My weapons include but are not limited to machine learning (deep learning), genomics technology, chemical engineering, and electronics.

My journey started from the macro level. I studied the fescue toxics-related cattle microbiome dynamics. Then I compared the microbiome of another big animal cohort. These works were mainly based on whole metagenome shotgun sequencing (WMGS). To make other WMGS reaches easier, I developed a popular open-source bioinformatics pipeline.
With these experiences, I began to investigate on the micro level. I designed a deep learning model to solve a microbiota metabolism problem. I’m currently working on a more comprehensive and complicated network regulation problem.

For the rest of my time, I tackled several other problems. One is cancer research, we applied single cell sequencing to the lung cancer brain metastases issue. Another is telomere research, I developed open-source software to deliver the largest scale of telomeric motif scanning in insects to date. I also researched brain-computer interface (BCI), I wrote tens of technological articles to help my ~1000 followers, with the assistance of my 3D printed BCI helmet.

If you have similar interests, please contact me. I’m looking for good cooperators.
